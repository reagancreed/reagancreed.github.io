About

I am a Plant Biology PhD candidate at the University of California, Davis (Cell & Developmental Biology, biotechnology emphasis). My work sits at the intersection of molecular genetics, seed biology, and quantitative analysis.

Before UC Davis, I studied Biology and Latin at Skidmore College and worked in research and teaching environments spanning greenhouse, field, microscopy, and computational analysis settings.

How I work

  • Experimental rigor: thoughtful controls, repeatable protocols, and clear interpretation boundaries.
  • Reproducible analysis: versioned, documented workflows in Python/R and HPC environments.
  • Team impact: mentoring trainees and building lab systems that improve consistency and throughput.

Interests

I am especially interested in translational plant biotech problems where rigorous biological evidence and practical implementation can accelerate crop innovation.
