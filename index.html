Plant Biology PhD Candidate · UC Davis

Seed biology, genetics, and computational workflows for reproducible research.

I’m Reagan Reed, a plant biotechnology researcher with 8+ years of research experience across molecular biology, seed science, and crop improvement. My current work integrates gene editing, developmental-stage phenotyping, and quantitative analysis to understand how seeds acquire quality traits such as dormancy behavior, rapid germination, and early vigor.

Current Research Focus

Gene editing for seed traits

CRISPR-based workflows in tomato to test seed dormancy, germination, and stress resilience hypotheses.

Seed phenotyping under stress

Greenhouse and controlled-condition experiments evaluating germination behavior under temperature and osmotic stress.

Computational analysis

Reproducible pipelines in Python/R and HPC-enabled RNA-seq analysis to support transparent, data-driven conclusions.

Selected Highlights

  • Led end-to-end PhD research programs in seed vigor and germination (UC Davis, 2020–2026 expected).
  • Helped establish and scale new lab operations; mentored 25+ trainees across levels.
  • Published in Heredity, Critical Reviews in Plant Sciences, Cell, and Frontiers in Plant Science.

Current framing of my dissertation work emphasizes a developmentally grounded view of seed quality acquisition: connecting timing, physiology, and molecular signals to practical outcomes for crop establishment.
